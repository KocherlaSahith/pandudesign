The Challenge

Agricultural carbon programs
face five systemic barriers

🗂️

Data Fragmentation

Each company uses its own tools and MRV formats, making state-level aggregation impossible.

📡

No Real-Time Monitoring

Monthly reports and manual surveys leave underperformance undetected until it's too late.

💰

Financial Opacity

No standardised cost tracking per hectare or tCO₂e makes benchmarking impossible.

⚠️

MRV Integrity Risks

Manual entry and lack of cross-validation risk registry rejection and credit invalidation.

🌾

Farmer Trust Deficit

Farmers can't see their carbon earnings or payment timeline, leading to high drop-off rates.

Architecture

Three-Layer Governance Model

Centralised intelligence, decentralised execution — each layer with defined authority, data flows, and accountability.

Layer
3

Governance — IIT Roorkee (IITR)

Acts as the central standard setter, MRV validator, data custodian, and benchmarking authority. Reviews and approves carbon calculations, sets methodology versions, and signs off on verified credits.

MRV Standards Carbon Sign-off Compliance Certificates Audit Authority
Layer
2

Intelligence — Open dMRV Platform Engine

The automated core: standardises data formats, runs MRV calculations, triggers satellite monitoring, manages the financial tracking layer, and fires the 3-tier alert escalation engine for anomalies.

Auto MRV Calculation Satellite Monitoring Financial Benchmarking Data Quality Scoring

1

Execution — Companies, Field Agents & Farmers

Implementing companies handle farmer onboarding, field operations, and activity logging. Field agents capture GPS evidence and consents. Farmers access earnings and payment status in real time.
