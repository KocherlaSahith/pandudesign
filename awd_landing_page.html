The Challenge

Five barriers blocking agricultural carbon at scale

Without a unified platform, these problems compound across every company, district, and growing season — making carbon programs impossible to trust or scale.

01

Data Fragmentation

Each company uses its own tools, formats, and MRV interpretation. State-level aggregation becomes impossible, preventing any meaningful benchmarking.

Unified schema enforcement + central data lake
02

No Real-Time Monitoring

Reliance on monthly reports and manual field surveys means underperformance goes undetected until it is far too late to course-correct.

Fortnightly satellite auto-monitoring + live dashboards
03

Financial Opacity

No standardised cost tracking per hectare, farmer, or tCO₂e makes efficient fund use and cross-company benchmarking an impossibility.

Financial tracking module with cost analytics
04

MRV Integrity Risks

Manual entry with no cross-validation risks registry rejection, credit invalidation, and permanent loss of institutional credibility.

AI cross-validation + 3-tier alert escalation engine
05

Farmer Trust Deficit

Farmers cannot see their carbon generated, revenue earned, or payment timeline — leading to high seasonal drop-off and low adoption.

Real-time farmer app + transparent payment workflow

Three-Layer Architecture

Centralised intelligence,
decentralised execution

Each layer has defined authority, data flows, and accountability — enforced by the platform.

Layer 01
Execution

Companies, Field Agents & Farmers

Implementing companies handle farmer onboarding, consent capture, and activity logging. Field agents record GPS evidence. Farmers access real-time earnings via app and SMS.

Farmer Onboarding Digital Consent Activity Logging
Layer 02
Intelligence

Open dMRV Platform Engine

The automated core standardises data formats, runs MRV calculations, triggers satellite monitoring, manages financial tracking, and fires the 3-tier alert escalation engine.

Auto MRV Satellite Monitoring Data Quality Scoring
Layer 03
Governance

IIT Roorkee — Central Authority

IITR sets MRV standards, reviews and approves carbon calculations, acts as data custodian and audit authority, and will serve as India's future national dMRV body.
